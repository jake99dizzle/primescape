About Prime Gaps

A prime gap is the distance between consecutive primes. After 7 comes 11 — gap of 4. After 23 comes 29 — gap of 6.

Twin primes (gap=2): pairs like 11,13 or 17,19. Possibly infinite — unproven.
Cousin primes (gap=4): pairs like 7,11 or 13,17.
Sexy primes (gap=6): named for the Latin sex. Pairs like 5,11 or 7,13.
Prime deserts: unusually long gaps — rarer and longer as p grows.

The gap histogram, π(x) curve, and Ulam spiral are visible when Show Math is on.

Largest known gap ≤10M: 154, between 2,010,733 and 2,010,881.

How to Use

Fly — the visualization runs automatically. You are flying forward through prime number space. Each form that blooms around you encodes a prime gap.

Tap anywhere on the main screen to burst a cluster of forms at that point.

Show Math (bottom-left button) — reveals four live panels: a stats card, gap histogram, π(x) prime-counting curve, and Ulam spiral.

Starting Number — slide to any region up to 10 million and tap "Begin journey here" to jump there instantly.

Color & Speed — change palette, flight speed, and dreaminess from this menu anytime.

Sound — ambient tones react to gap size. Best with headphones.
